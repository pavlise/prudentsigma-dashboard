Business Overview

Nike, Inc. is the world's largest athletic footwear and apparel company, operating globally across North America, Europe, Greater China, and Asia Pacific. The company is undergoing a significant turnaround under CEO Elliott Hill (appointed Oct 2024), who replaced John Donahoe and launched the "Win Now" five-pillar strategy focused on sport-centric product innovation, wholesale channel restoration, and operational restructuring. Revenue declined 10% in FY2025 to $46.3B and continues to face headwinds from tariffs (~$1.5B annualised cost impact) and a deepening China contraction. However, North America is recovering (+9% in Q2 FY2026), inventory is being cleaned up, and the stock is near a 12-year low — potentially pricing in excessive pessimism.

Key Risks

  • Tariff Structural Risk: $1.5B annualised gross margin headwind — not easily offset short-term; Vietnam/Indonesia supply chains take 2-3 years to meaningfully shift
  • China Impairment: Revenue -17% in FY2025, -7% Q3 FY2026, -20% guided Q4 FY2026. Local brands (Anta, Li-Ning) and Adidas have taken durable share
  • Execution Risk on Turnaround: Elliott Hill's "Win Now" strategy requires 2-3 year execution horizon; multiple consecutive revenue declines could erode brand equity
  • Competitive Pressure: On Running, HOKA, New Balance, Adidas all taking premium running/casual share; Nike's DTC retrenchment hurt direct relationships with core customers
  • Margin Recovery Timeline: CFO explicitly stated margin improvement unlikely before Q2 FY2027 at the earliest
  • Macro Sensitivity: Athletic footwear is discretionary; recession would disproportionately hit Nike's mid-to-premium price points
