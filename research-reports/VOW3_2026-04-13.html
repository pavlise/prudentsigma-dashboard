Business Overview

Volkswagen AG is Europe's largest automaker by volume and a global top-3 by revenue (~€317B in FY2025), operating 12 brands including VW, Audi, Porsche, ŠKODA, SEAT, Lamborghini, and Bentley. The group is navigating one of the most severe structural crises in its history: FY2025 operating profit collapsed ~51% to ~€9.4B (4.6% margin), driven by a rapid China market share loss to local EV brands, $1.5B+ US tariff headwinds, and a delayed EV profitability ramp. Management announced 50,000 German job cuts by 2030 and a €6B+ annual savings target. However, the Automotive Division retained a net cash buffer of €34B, and FY2025 automotive net cash flow of ~€6B beat expectations — providing a meaningful margin of safety. At a P/E of ~4–6x and a 6%+ dividend yield, the market is pricing in structural failure.

Key Risks

  • China Structural Decline: Q1 2026 -15%; BYD and 20+ local EV brands have taken durable share in the world's largest auto market where VW derives ~30% of profits
  • US Tariff Barrier: Q1 2026 US deliveries -20.5%; VW admitted its North America model "no longer works as intended" — structural reset required
  • EV Transition Cost: Massive capex for EV platforms (MEB, SSP) without yet matching ICE profitability; Porsche already delaying parts of EV transition
  • German Labour Rigidity: 50,000 job cut programme faces union resistance; restructuring charges will suppress near-term earnings (FY2026-2027)
  • CO2 Fines (Europe): If BEV mix falls short of EU 2025 targets, VW faces multi-billion euro fines — a direct hit to cash flow
  • Pension Obligations: Large German pension liabilities (~€20-25B est.) reduce effective equity value and can grow with demographic and interest rate changes

Conclusion: Volkswagen appears undervalued at €88.24 on a DCF basis, with a recovery fair value of ~€109.5/share (+24% upside) and a ~6.1% dividend yield that pays investors to wait. The stock trades at 4-6x P/E and below the most pessimistic DCF scenario — pricing in near-total failure of the restructuring programme. With €34B in automotive net cash, a credible cost-savings plan, and BEV deliveries accelerating (+32% in FY2025), the margin of safety is substantial. The primary risks — China and US tariffs — are real but are partly beyond management control and may resolve in the medium term. VW is a deep-value play with a generous dividend floor, suited to patient investors with a 3-5 year horizon who can tolerate ongoing headline risk during the restructuring phase.
