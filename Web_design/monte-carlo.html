Step 02

Configure hedging strategies

Select strategies to compare

Select one or more strategies. The simulator will run each in parallel and present a side-by-side comparison.

Strategy A
Full Internalization

Firm absorbs 100% of client exposure. No hedges placed — pure P&L from market moves over the full holding period. Maximum risk, zero hedge cost.

Strategy B
Periodic Hedging

Firm hedges accumulated net exposure at fixed time intervals, then re-internalizes new flow. Balances market risk against hedging frequency costs.

Hedge interval min
Strategy C
VaR Boundary Hedging

Firm hedges when unrealized P&L breaches a defined VaR threshold. A risk-limit trigger that adapts hedge frequency to actual market conditions.

Monte Carlo simulation using Geometric Brownian Motion with user-defined annualised volatility per instrument. Volatility is scaled to the chosen time horizon: σ_T = σ_annual × √(T/252). Strategy A: firm holds full net position for duration T. Strategy B: firm holds position for T/m (where m = T ÷ interval), then hedges — one spread execution cost. Strategy C: path-simulated over 50 fine steps; hedge triggered whenever |unrealised P&L| ≥ threshold. Strategy D: deterministic — zero market risk, spread cost only. VaR = 5th percentile of simulated P&L. CVaR = mean of worst 5% of outcomes. Ruin probability = % of simulations where loss exceeds capital buffer.
