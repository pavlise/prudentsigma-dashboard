Dominant Macro Theme Today:

announced a two-week suspension of military operations against Iran, with Tehran
pledging to reopen the Strait of Hormuz — the choke point responsible for ~20%
of global oil supply. This single event is reshaping every cross-asset signal
simultaneously: crude oil futures are plunging post-open, equities futures are

safe-haven bid in gold is softening at the margin. With March CPI releasing
tomorrow (April 10), the market faces a compressed sequence of catalysts that
could either cement or reverse this pre-market rally within 48 hours.

  elevated put buying — the options market remains defensively positioned despite
  the bullish pre-market futures. This creates a potential short-squeeze dynamic

suggesting investors are reducing defensive positioning as the ceasefire shifts
the probability distribution toward a soft-landing scenario. Today's open is
likely to amplify this rotation further: airlines, consumer discretionary, and
tech should see outsized inflows.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — March CPI (April 10):
This is the single most consequential data point in the near-term window. March
CPI captures the first full month of Iran-conflict energy impact, when gasoline
prices spiked sharply. Pre-ceasefire, the base case was a hot print (+0.3-0.4%
MoM core) that would have crushed rate-cut hopes. The ceasefire announcement
changes the calculus significantly: if energy was the primary driver of March
inflation, the Fed may look through it as a transitory geopolitical shock
(analogous to the 2022 Ukraine energy spike). The market is positioned bearishly
(P/C > 1.40). An in-line or soft print (+0.2% core MoM) could ignite a
significant squeeze — CME FedWatch already shows 45% odds of at least one cut by
year-end, up sharply today. A hot print (+0.4%+ core) would be structurally
bearish regardless of the ceasefire, pressuring the 10Y yield toward 4.50% and
the SMA200 reclaim scenario for SPX. **Asymmetric risk: benign CPI = explosive
rally; hot CPI = test of April lows.**
