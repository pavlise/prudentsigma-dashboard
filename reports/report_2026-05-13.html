near-perfection at a moment of maximum transition risk.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — April CPI (May 13, 08:30 ET)

Expected: Headline +0.6% MoM / +3.7% YoY | Core +0.3% MoM / +2.7% YoY.
This is the defining number for the week. The 10Y yield has already repriced
+4.86% in a month, approaching the 52W high of 4.60%. An inline or soft print
would confirm disinflation progress and validate the rally — equity bulls need
core CPI to not surprise to the upside. The bear case is acute: energy prices
(WTI +75% YTD) feed with a lag into headline, and shelter inflation has been
sticky. A hot headline above +4.0% YoY would stress the 10Y through 4.60%,
compress equity multiples at RSI-overbought levels, and potentially hand Warsh
an impossible mandate on Day 1. Asymmetric risk is to the downside on equities
if CPI surprises. Options market is explicitly pricing this via QQQ P/C of 1.94.

The Warsh transition (May 15) adds a second-order risk: his stated desire to
end forward guidance means markets can no longer anchor on Fed signals. Paul
Tudor Jones publicly stated "no chance" of rate cuts under Warsh in 2026. If
that view gains traction, the 10Y could trade to 4.75–5.00%, compressing
P/E multiples on growth stocks that only recently recovered.
