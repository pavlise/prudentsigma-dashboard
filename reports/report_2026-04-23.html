HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — "Super Wednesday" April 29 (FOMC + Mag-4 Earnings):

This is the single most significant day in markets in months. The FOMC is
universally expected to hold rates at 3.50–3.75%, but the press conference
carries real asymmetric risk. Chair Powell faces a stagflationary squeeze:
March core PCE is tracking toward ~3.1% annualized (well above the 2% target)
while the ceasefire-driven oil decline offers relief — but the ceasefire is
only two weeks old and unresolved Strait of Hormuz toll disputes could reignite
pressure. A hawkish hold (Powell emphasizes inflation persistence, rules out
June cuts) risks a sharp tech/duration unwind. A dovish hold (Powell emphasizes
oil disinflation, signals flexibility for H2 cuts) extends the melt-up.

The simultaneous Mag-4 earnings amplify the binary. Microsoft (Azure guide
37-38% growth), Alphabet (~$107B revenue expected), Meta (~$55.5B revenue,
$7.51 EPS), and Amazon face collective scrutiny on whether AI monetization
justifies AI capex (Meta's $115-135B FY plan). A single big miss — or guidance
cut — could cascade through a sector that already trades at RSI 74. Conversely,
a clean sweep of beats with raised guidance could push NDX to 28,000+.

Positioning: Consider asymmetric protection (NDX put spreads) heading into
April 28. The risk/reward of being flat or lightly short tech into Super
Wednesday is better than chasing an RSI 74 market.
