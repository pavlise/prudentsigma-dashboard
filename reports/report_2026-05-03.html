HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — April NFP (Friday, May 8):
March NFP came in at +178K, a dramatic reversal from February's -133K and nearly 3×
the +60K consensus. The market will be watching to see whether that rebound was a
one-month anomaly or the start of a re-acceleration. With the April 28–29 FOMC holding
at 3.50–3.75% and the statement noting "elevated inflation" from energy prices, the Fed
is in a stagflation-adjacent bind — Powell's final act explicitly signaled no cuts in
2026 (June cut probability: ~5%). A strong NFP (>150K) would cement the "higher for
longer" narrative, likely steepening the 10Y toward 4.50–4.60% and pressuring
rate-sensitive sectors (Real Estate, Utilities). A weak print (<75K) would reignite
cut expectations and could spike VIX as recession fears re-emerge. The asymmetry is
skewed bearish: if the number disappoints and 10Y yields spike simultaneously (i.e.,
stagflation read), equities at ATH have significant retracement risk. Watch the ADP
Wednesday print as a directional tell.
