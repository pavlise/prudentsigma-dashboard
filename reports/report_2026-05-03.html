Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The defining force in global markets remains the Middle East energy shock. Since Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz in late February, Gulf OPEC producers have shut in approximately 9.1 million barrels per day, catapulting WTI crude +78% YTD to ~$102. Against this backdrop, the April 29 FOMC meeting delivered a hawkish hold — rates unchanged at 3.50–3.75%, with an unprecedented 8-4 split — as dissenting hawks (Hammack, Kashkari, Logan) pushed back against any easing bias embedded in the statement. Fed Chair Powell confirmed he will step down while remaining as governor, adding institutional uncertainty. Equities have defied this backdrop, led by a monster AI/tech earnings season, but overbought momentum signals and elevated QQQ put/call ratios suggest the rally is approaching a decision point.

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: SPX and NDX sit above their SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200 — textbook bull structure. WTI is similarly above all three MAs (+47.8% above SMA200). However, Gold and DXY present a bearish divergence: Gold has broken *below* its SMA20 and SMA50 after peaking near $5,318, while DXY trades below all three MAs. This tells a nuanced story: the "safe haven" bid is rotating *out* of gold and *into* energy as the inflation driver, not fear-driven flight.

- Momentum: The headline divergence is NDX RSI at 75 and SPX at 71 — both in overbought territory after a +15%/+10% surge over the past month. Gold RSI at 45 and Silver at 51 are neutral-to-mildly bearish, consistent with their consolidation phase. Critically, WTI RSI holds at 56 with MACD bullish — momentum is not yet exhausted on crude despite the massive YTD run. BTC at RSI 60 with bearish MACD suggests upside momentum is fading near SMA200 resistance.

- Volatility: VIX at 16.99 signals complacency — a -28.82% collapse over the past month. The VIX term structure in contango (spot 16.99 vs. 3M 20.37) shows markets pricing for short-term calm but structural uncertainty ahead. WTI ATR% at 7.5% dominates cross-asset volatility, while NDX ATR at 1.41% reflects high daily range with overbought conditions. The Bollinger Band %B for NDX at 0.84 (near upper band) confirms a stretched rally with limited near-term upside buffer.

- Cross-asset signals: The SPX/HYG correlation at 0.88 is the key credit-equity alignment — HYG is holding (+1.16% 1M), indicating no credit stress yet. The critical watch is whether HYG diverges from equities; any widening credit spread would be an early warning. Gold/DXY correlation at -0.27 (weakly negative, unusual) suggests gold is partially decoupling from dollar dynamics, driven by the Iran energy shock rather than pure USD moves.

- Options sentiment: QQQ put/call volume ratio at 2.38 stands out as a sharp bearish lean — suggesting institutional hedging or directional bets against the tech rally. SPY P/C at 1.17 is neutral, implying the hedging is concentrated specifically in mega-cap tech. Combined with VIX contango, the market is not panicking but is paying up to protect tech longs.

Sector Rotation: XLK (Tech) is the runaway leader at +19.03% over the past month (+9.20% relative to SPX), powered by earnings beats across AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, META, GOOGL, and AMZN. XLE (Energy) is the glaring underperformer at -0.68% 1M, down -10.51% *relative to SPX* — a major divergence from $102 WTI crude. This suggests energy producers are being penalized for cost pressures and geopolitical risk to their operations, not rewarded for higher oil prices. Defensives (XLP, XLU, XLV) are lagging broadly, consistent with a risk-on positioning in equities despite macro headwinds.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — April NFP (Friday May 8):
This is the most consequential print of the week and possibly the month. With the FOMC in a hawkish hold and the 8-4 dissent signaling internal division, a hot payroll number (+200K+, unemployment below 4.0%) would reinforce the hawkish dissenting bloc (Hammack/Kashkari/Logan) and further compress any probability of a June cut, sending the 10Y yield toward its 4.60% resistance and pressuring equities. Conversely, a soft print (<150K, unemployment uptick) would validate Miran's case for a cut, likely triggering a relief rally in tech and a DXY selloff. The asymmetric risk: the bond market is currently pricing minimal cuts in 2026; a weak NFP would be a *larger* positive surprise to equities than a hot print would be a negative, given how much hawkishness is already embedded. Market positioning appears neutral-to-cautious (SPY P/C 1.17), suggesting room for a sharp move in either direction.
