SECTION 2 — TECHNICAL SNAPSHOT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Unambiguously bullish in equities and precious metals.
  SPX and NDX are trading above SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200 simultaneously — a
  full bull alignment. Gold also holds its SMA200 by a 16% margin. WTI is the
  outlier: -14.2% below SMA20 and -0.7% below SMA50 — a bearish structure
  forming after the oil shock peak. BTC sits in a bear structure vs. its SMA200
  (86,791) despite short-term momentum.

- Momentum: Equity RSIs are flashing overbought warnings. SPX at 73 and
  NDX at 74 are entering the "risk zone" where historically >75% of the time
  a pullback of 3–5% materialises within 2–3 weeks. Silver (RSI 59) and BTC
  (RSI 67) have materially more momentum runway. Copper's RSI at 68 confirms
  broad industrial demand remains intact, reinforcing the growth narrative.
  WTI (RSI 42) and Crude's bearish MACD are consistent with continued supply
  relief and a de-escalation premium being unwound.

- Volatility: VIX at 17.48 sits well below its 52W high of 33.82 — the
  fear of March has been priced away. The VIX term structure is in contango
  (VIX3M at 20.51 vs. spot 17.48), signalling that options markets expect
  near-term calm but build in elevated vol into summer — consistent with
  earnings season uncertainty. WTI's ATR% of 10.44% is the highest across all
  tracked assets, confirming crude remains the most volatile instrument in the
  universe. SPX ATR% at just 1.29% suggests the index is in a compressed,
  low-vol state that typically precedes a sharp directional move.

- Cross-asset signals: The Gold/DXY correlation remains near zero (+0.06
  rolling) — a positive divergence from the classical inverse relationship.
  This signals gold is being driven by geopolitical and inflation demand rather
  than pure dollar weakness — a structurally more powerful and durable driver.
  The SPX/HYG correlation at 0.87 confirms credit and equities are aligned (no
  early credit stress forming). The BTC/SPX correlation at 0.52 remains
  elevated, meaning crypto is still trading as a risk asset, not a hedge.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C volume at 1.37 and OI at 1.97 indicate the
  market is carrying a significant put overhang. This is a dual-edged signal:
  elevated put ownership provides a potential short-squeeze if markets rally
  further, but also signals institutional hedging of a regime that participants
  do not fully trust at these extended levels.

Sector Rotation: Technology (XLK) is the clear leader, outperforming SPX
by +3.77% on the month — institutional capital is flowing into high-growth,
AI-exposed names. Energy (XLE) is the most conspicuous laggard at -14.57%
relative to SPX over the month, directly reflecting the WTI collapse from its
Iran-shock highs. Financials (XLF) are broadly neutral, consistent with a
"rates on hold" narrative. Healthcare and Utilities are bleeding defensively —
risk appetite is unambiguously real-money risk-on despite the put-buying overlay.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — Q1 Earnings Season + Core PCE (Fri Apr 25):
The dual catalyst of the earnings avalanche and Friday's Core PCE print
represents the binary event risk for the week. On earnings: the S&P 500 is
running its 6th consecutive quarter of double-digit YoY EPS growth, and all 11
sectors are projecting positive revenue growth. Alphabet and Tesla (Wed) will
set the tone — any miss in cloud/AI spend guidance from Alphabet would reprice
the Tech trade that has driven XLK's +11.64% 1M outperformance. On PCE: the
Fed revised its 2026 PCE forecast to 2.7% in March, citing oil-driven
inflationary pressure from the Iran conflict. A PCE print above 2.8% MoM
would essentially eliminate the single cut the Fed is projecting for 2026,
triggering a bond selloff, 10Y yield spike above 4.40%, and likely a 2–4%
equity correction from overbought levels. The asymmetric risk is to the downside
on a PCE upside surprise at these stretched valuations.

Additional context — Kevin Warsh Testimony: The newly nominated Fed Chair's
Senate confirmation testimony this week carries tail-risk: any signal of a more
hawkish policy stance than Powell's "one-cut" baseline could spike real yields
and pressure equities.
