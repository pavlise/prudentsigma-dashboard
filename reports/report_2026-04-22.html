Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: SPX and NDX are above SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200 — a
  clean bull structure. NDX is outperforming (+6.7% above SMA50 vs SPX at +4.2%)
  signaling tech-led leadership. WTI is the notable exception: below SMA20,
  reflecting the rapid geopolitical deflation of the crude risk-premium. BTC
  sits in a structurally bearish posture (below SMA200 at 86,116) despite strong
  short-term momentum, which means the recovery rally is best treated as
  tactical, not structural.

- Momentum: NDX RSI has crossed 70 — the first overbought reading since the
  current rally began. SPX RSI at 67 has room but is approaching the same
  threshold. Critically, Gold RSI sits at a neutral 51 despite a +10.88% YTD
  move, suggesting this is not a frothy momentum chase but a regime re-rating.
  Silver RSI at 52 and WTI at 46 both point to mean-reversion potential in
  commodities — but in opposite directions.

- Volatility: VIX contango (spot 19.50 vs VIX3M at 21.51) is normal and
  non-alarming, but the 5D spike (+7.32% in VIX alongside +0.58% in SPX) is a
  classic divergence signal — options markets hedging event risk (Tesla/GOOGL
  earnings today; PMI data Thursday) even as the underlying grinds higher. ATR%
  is elevated in WTI (9.33%) and silver (4.46%), flagging outsized intraday
  swings in these instruments.

- Cross-asset signals: The SPX/HYG correlation is holding at 0.87 — credit
  is confirming the equity rally, which is constructive. More notable: Gold/DXY
  correlation is near zero (-0.04), meaning gold's bid is NOT purely USD-
  driven — it reflects genuine safe-haven and geopolitical demand, making it
  more durable. DXY below all 3 moving averages in a clear bear trend structurally
  supports gold, silver, EM assets, and multinationals with overseas revenue.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C ratio at 1.20 (neutral) but QQQ at 1.35
  (elevated put buying) shows institutional hedging against the very tech rally
  they're riding. This is classic "buy the rumor, hedge the news" positioning
  ahead of today's TSLA/GOOGL prints. A strong beat could force a put unwind
  (gamma squeeze higher); a miss could accelerate a mean-reversion move.

Sector Rotation: XLK (Tech) is dominating this week with +12.95% over one
month (+5.61% relative to SPX). XLE (Energy) is the laggard at -6.31% (1M)
vs SPX, consistent with WTI's 5D decline. XLRE (Real Estate) is quietly
outperforming (+7.78% 1M, +0.44% relative) — a tell that bond proxies are
catching a bid as the 10Y yield retreats. Healthcare (XLV) and Utilities (XLU)
are both -6.5% relative over a month, suggesting this is a risk-on rotation,
not a defensive one. Institutional flows are clearly chasing AI infrastructure
and rate-sensitive real assets.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — Tesla Q1 2026 Earnings (Today, post-close):
Consensus expects $23.06B revenue (+19% YoY) and adj. EPS of $0.40 (+48% YoY),
but the delivery pre-print came in at 358,023 units vs. 365,645 expected — a
2.1% miss. This removes the top-line upside surprise from the table. The real
binary is margin guidance and the Cybercab/FSD monetization update. If TSLA
guides FY margins above 17% and confirms Cybercab production start dates, the
stock likely rallies 8–12%; if margins disappoint and Tesla reaffirms delivery
softness, expect a 10–15% decline. Alphabet carries less event risk — AI search
revenue beats have been consistent, and $175–185B in 2026 capex guidance is
already priced. The aggregate signal from both prints will set the NDX tone for
the remainder of the week.
