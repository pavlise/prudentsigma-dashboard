Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The defining narrative of April 2026 is the "Great Rotation" — a structural
regime shift driven by three converging forces: re-accelerating inflation
(CPI re-accelerating toward 3.4%), a hawkish Fed pause (rate cuts officially
shelved for 2026), and a Middle East energy shock via the Strait of Hormuz
blockade that sent WTI surging +60% YTD to $91.72 — well below its $112.95
52W high but still elevated. The IMF yesterday cut its 2026 global growth
forecast to 3.1% (from 3.3%) citing the energy shock and rising inflation,
with an adverse scenario showing growth at just 2.5%. Meanwhile, positive
signals are emerging: Iran talks are reportedly resuming, and this morning's
Q1 earnings from Bank of America and Morgan Stanley beat estimates — an
early green shoot for earnings season.

SECTION 2 — TECHNICAL SNAPSHOT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Equities remain in a textbook bull structure. SPX trades
  +4.8% above its SMA200 (6,674) and +3.5% above SMA50 (6,761), confirming the
  uptrend is intact. NDX mirrors this with all three MAs below price. Notably,
  the DXY sits below ALL three moving averages (SMA20, SMA50, SMA200) — a pure
  bear structure, amplifying the tailwind for dollar-denominated commodities.
  BTC is the key outlier: above SMA20/SMA50 but -15.2% below SMA200 ($87,344),
  placing it in a bear-trend recovery phase rather than a new bull cycle.

- Momentum: NDX RSI at 70 is the most actionable signal on the board —
  borderline overbought, with price sitting above the upper Bollinger Band
  (BB% = 1.01). SPX RSI at 68 is approaching, but not yet, overbought. In
  contrast, DXY RSI at 38 is approaching oversold, suggesting the dollar sell-
  off may be nearing a short-term bounce. Gold RSI at 54 is neutral, consistent
  with healthy consolidation near SMA50 after the parabolic move from $3,181.
  Silver at RSI 56 and Copper at RSI 68 (BB% = 1.03, above upper band) signal
  industrial metals running hot.

- Volatility: VIX at 17.95 in a contango structure (VIX3M = 20.73) signals
  the market is pricing in more uncertainty further out — a rational posture
  given Iran geopolitics and Fed uncertainty. WTI ATR% at 9.38% is the highest
  on the board, reflecting the energy market's extreme sensitivity to headline
  risk. Equity ATRs (SPX 1.33%, NDX 1.65%) are elevated but not extreme.

- Cross-asset signals: The Gold/DXY 30-day correlation is nearly flat at
  -0.07 — significantly less negative than the historical norm. This means gold's
  strength is NOT purely a dollar story; it reflects genuine safe-haven demand
  and inflation hedging. SPX/HYG correlation of 0.88 shows credit and equities
  remain tightly aligned — no stress fractures in credit markets yet. The
  WTI/DXY positive correlation (+0.58) is unusual (typically negative) and
  reflects geopolitical risk premium being priced into both assets simultaneously.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C volume ratio at 0.63 and QQQ at 0.49 both
  signal call-dominated flow — a bullish tilt in near-term positioning. However,
  the SPY P/C OI ratio of 2.22 (high put open interest) suggests institutions
  are running significant tail-risk hedges beneath the surface. This is a classic
  "buy the dip, hedge the tail" posture ahead of key earnings and geopolitical
  catalysts.

Sector Rotation: Tech (XLK) is the undisputed leader: +4.85% over 5 days
(+2.33% vs SPX) and +6.88% over 1 month (+2.70% vs SPX). Financials (XLF)
are modestly outperforming on the back of earnings beats from GS, BAC, and MS.
The notable surprise: Energy (XLE) is underperforming SPX by -5.02% over 5
days despite WTI's +60% YTD move — the market is pricing in Iran deal risk and
mean reversion in crude. Defensives (XLP, XLV, XLU) are all lagging, consistent
with a risk-on, growth-leaning rotation. This sector configuration confirms the
bullish tilt but flags that energy bulls may be swimming against institutional flows.
