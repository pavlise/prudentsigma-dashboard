Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The two-week U.S.-Iran ceasefire announced on April 7 has triggered a broad
relief rally: equities surged, WTI crude fell ~15% from peak war-premium
levels, and Treasury yields pulled back. However, the ceasefire is explicitly
temporary and Iran has not yet fully reopened the Strait of Hormuz — leaving a
fat tail of geopolitical risk priced into oil and a skeptical options market
still buying puts. Simultaneously, Trump's April 2026 global tariff package (10%
baseline, up to 34% on Asia) is pushing the Fed into a corner — rate-cut odds
have recovered to ~45% year-end but structural inflationary pressure remains.
Today's March CPI print (8:30 AM ET) is the day's pivotal data point.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — March CPI (April 10, 8:30 AM ET):

This is the single most important data point of the week, releasing today. The
March reading will be the first CPI to capture partial tariff pass-through to
consumer prices. Consensus is watching for a MoM uptick driven by imported
goods, particularly consumer electronics (facing 25-34% tariffs on Asian
manufacturing). A hot print above 0.4% MoM would confirm stagflation fears,
force the Fed to signal higher-for-longer, and likely reprice rate-cut odds
below 30% — a material headwind for rate-sensitive equities and credit.
Conversely, a cool reading (0.2% or below) would validate the ceasefire
narrative, confirm energy disinflation from WTI's $15+ pullback, and send
equities toward the 52W high of 6,978. The asymmetric risk: a hot print
breaks more than a cool print gains, given SPX is already at BB%B=0.99.
Position size accordingly — this is a morning event, and directional bets
ahead of the number carry binary risk.

For the PPI on April 14 — tariff pass-through at the producer level will be
even more telling for corporate margin outlook. A PPI beat could start a wave
of earnings guidance cuts heading into Q1 reporting season.
