  NDX (74) are deep in overbought territory, yet MACD remains bullish on
  both — historically, this combination can persist 5-10 sessions before
  mean reversion. Silver (RSI 59) and Copper (RSI 68) are elevated but NOT
  overbought, suggesting more runway in the commodity complex. WTI (RSI 42)
  and DXY (RSI 42) are approaching oversold territory — watch for
  technical bounces that could be faded.

- Volatility: VIX at 17.48 in contango (VIX3M at 20.51) signals
  orderly risk appetite rather than complacency. ATR% is notable: Silver
  at 4.54% and WTI at 10.44% are the highest-volatility assets in the
  complex — position sizing must account for this. SPX ATR% at 1.29%
  is relatively contained. Bollinger Band compression across equity
  indices (BB% 0.98-0.99) historically precedes either a breakout
  continuation or a sharp reversal — the TSLA and mega-cap prints
  will be the deciding catalyst.

  firmly — no credit stress visible, which validates the equity rally.
  The DXY/Gold correlation at -0.06 (nearly zero) is a significant signal:
  gold is no longer being driven purely by dollar weakness but by structural
  de-dollarization and central bank demand — a more durable bull case.
  Gold/Silver 30D correlation at 0.88 confirms the entire precious metals
  complex is moving in unison.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C volume at 1.02 (neutral) but QQQ P/C
  volume at 1.46 signals elevated put buying in tech ahead of earnings —
  the market is hedged, not complacent, into the TSLA print. Open interest
  remains skewed bearish (SPY OI 1.76, QQQ OI 1.56), suggesting a
  positive earnings surprise could trigger a meaningful short-squeeze.

Sector Rotation: XLK (Tech) is the clear institutional favorite —
+11.64% in one month vs. SPX's +7.86%, a +3.77% relative outperformance.
XLRE (Real Estate) is a quiet second at +6.82% 1M, benefiting from the
10Y yield decline. XLE (Energy) is the biggest loser at -6.71% 1M

energy sector into underperform. XLP (Staples) and XLV (Healthcare) are
also lagging sharply, consistent with a risk-on rotation out of defensives
and into growth.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — Tesla Q1 2026 Earnings (April 22, AMC):
Tesla is the week's most binary event. Q1 deliveries already printed
and came in below consensus, creating a low bar for EPS but raising
questions about margin compression amid the EV price war and supply
chain disruptions. Analyst consensus sits near $0.47 EPS. Any beat
— particularly on automotive gross margins above 13% — could ignite
a sharp short-squeeze, given elevated QQQ put positioning. Conversely,
a revenue miss combined with weak guidance on Cybertruck ramp or
energy storage will reprice the entire EV/growth complex. Watch for
CEO commentary on tariff impacts on China operations and the timeline
for the robotaxi launch — these will be the narrative catalysts that
matter far more than the headline EPS. Asymmetric risk is skewed to
the upside given the low expectations baseline, but the stop on any
tech long should be tightened before the print.

The Q1 GDP Advance Estimate (Friday) is the week's macro
wildcard. A print below 1.5% would ignite recession fears given tariff
drag; above 2.5% could reignite Fed hawkishness and pressure the bond
rally. The market currently expects modest deceleration from Q4 2025.
