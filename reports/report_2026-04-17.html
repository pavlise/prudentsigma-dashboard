SECTION 1 — MACRO PULSE (5-MINUTE READ)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The 2026 Iran War and U.S. naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz — now in
its sixth week — remains the defining macro force, having caused the largest
oil supply disruption in recorded history (~13M bpd offline). Markets are
exhibiting a cautious but resilient posture: equities are recovering sharply
(SPX +3.3% over 5 days), VIX has collapsed from its 33.82 peak to 17.94,
and crude oil is actually pulling back on whispers of a diplomatic off-ramp.
The macro puzzle is stagflationary: supply-shock inflation (WTI +56% YTD)
colliding with a Fed that is on hold, gifted by a surprisingly soft PPI
print that gives policymakers cover to neither hike nor cut aggressively.
Dollar weakness (DXY below all SMAs) and soaring precious metals confirm
markets are repricing global reserve dynamics in real time.

SECTION 2 — TECHNICAL SNAPSHOT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Trend Structure:
Both SPX and NDX are cleanly above their SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200 — a full
bull structure — after the violent recovery from the early-April lows. WTI
is the notable exception: price broke below SMA20 (97.96) on the back of
diplomatic-off-ramp newsflow, while remaining above SMA50 and SMA200.
BTC is in a mixed structure — bull on short-term MAs, but below SMA200
at 87,160, making it technically a "bear rally" until that level reclaims.

Momentum:
SPX (RSI 70) and NDX (RSI 72) are in overbought territory simultaneously —
the last time both crossed 70 together was the January 2026 peak. This does
not signal an immediate reversal but strongly argues against chasing new
longs at current levels. Silver (RSI 55) and BTC (RSI 62) retain more
momentum runway. WTI (RSI 46) is neutral-to-bearish, consistent with
the price break below SMA20 and MACD bearish crossover.

Volatility:
VIX at 17.94 vs. VIX3M at 20.77 = healthy contango. The market is pricing
near-term calm but maintaining a premium for medium-term uncertainty (Iran
negotiations outcome). ATR% is instructive: WTI (9.07%) and Silver (4.45%)
are the most volatile assets — position sizing must be scaled accordingly.
SPX ATR of 1.29% means a daily 90-point swing is within normal range.

Cross-Asset Signals:
The most important divergence is the SPX/HYG relationship: both are moving
together (30-day correlation 0.88, HYG +1.69% MTD), which confirms the
equity rally has credit market endorsement — no stealth stress forming.
The DXY/Gold de-coupling (correlation near 0; normally deeply negative) is
the outlier signal of the week: gold is no longer a dollar trade — it is
a geopolitical/stagflation hedge, and that makes its floor much harder to
break. Gold's near-term ceiling is SMA50 at 4,893; a close above that opens
the path toward the 52W high of 5,318.

Options Sentiment:
SPY P/C at 0.94 and QQQ P/C at 1.15 — both Neutral. The options market is
not heavily positioned for either a breakout or a breakdown. This is
consistent with a market that has absorbed the initial shock and is now
waiting for the next catalyst (Iran negotiations, upcoming earnings, Fed).

Sector Rotation:
XLK (Tech) is the dominant outperformer this week: +6.59% vs. SPX +3.29%,
a +330bps relative edge. TSMC just reported Q1 2026 EPS +58% YoY, and AI
infrastructure spending appears insulated from the geopolitical turbulence.
XLE (Energy) is the week's notable laggard: -0.63% vs. SPX +3.29% (-392bps),
a counter-intuitive underperformance given the oil supply shock — the market
is front-running the diplomatic off-ramp narrative. Defensives (XLP, XLU,
XLV) are all lagging the broad market by 370–460bps on the week, confirming
the risk-on character of the current move despite the macro backdrop.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — Tesla (TSLA) Q1 2026 Earnings | April 22 AMC

Tesla reports after the bell on Wednesday. Given NDX's RSI at 72 and the
entire tech recovery resting on AI/EV momentum, TSLA is a binary catalyst.
Consensus expects margin recovery from the Cybertruck ramp and FSD licensing
revenue, but delivery volumes in Q1 were pressured by the same supply chain
disruptions hitting global manufacturing. A beat on margins with forward
guidance affirming FSD monetization would be a significant NDX tailwind.
A miss — particularly on gross margins — risks a sharp reversal in an
already overbought NDX. Asymmetric risk skews SLIGHTLY bearish given the
overbought setup: good news is more priced-in than bad. Options premiums
on TSLA will be elevated into the print; IV crush post-earnings can be
exploited via short straddle structures for experienced traders.
