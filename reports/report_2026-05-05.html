Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis remains the single most important macro force in markets. Iran has blocked shipping since late February following U.S.-Israeli strikes, stranding Gulf oil exports and triggering what the IEA describes as *"the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market."* On May 4, Iran explicitly warned the U.S. against its "Operation Project Freedom" escort mission — escalation risk is live and front-and-center. Meanwhile, the FOMC held rates at 3.50–3.75% on April 29 in a contentious 4-dissenter vote, with the Fed retaining a conditional easing bias even as energy-driven inflation re-accelerates. This stagflationary mix — roaring oil, sticky inflation, rate pause — is the dominant regime shaping every cross-asset signal today.

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Equities remain in clean bull structure — SPX and NDX are above their SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200 simultaneously. WTI Crude is the most structurally extended asset (+51.5% above SMA200), reflecting the supply shock rather than economic demand. Gold and Silver have deteriorated: both are below their SMA20 and SMA50, signaling a corrective phase within a longer-term bull trend. BTC sits in an ambiguous position — above SMA20 and SMA50 but below SMA200 at 83,549; it is technically still in a bear structure on the daily.

- Momentum: NDX RSI at 73 is the standout alert — it is the only major asset in overbought territory, and its 14.3% 1-month surge has not been matched by breadth confirmation. SPX at RSI 68 is approaching, but not yet at, overbought. Notably, Gold (RSI 41) and Silver (RSI 45) are approaching oversold territory after sharp corrections from 52W highs of $5,318 and $115 respectively — a potential mean-reversion setup if Hormuz escalates into a full-scale commodity panic.

- Volatility: VIX at 18.29 is in contango vs. VIX3M at 21.05, indicating the market expects *more* volatility in 3 months than today — a structurally cautious read. WTI ATR% at 6.98% is the highest across all tracked assets, confirming crude as the most dangerous instrument to trade right now. NDX ATR% at 1.40% vs. SPX at 1.07% shows tech leading both returns and risk. Bitcoin at 2.66% ATR is elevated but manageable vs. prior regime.

- Cross-asset signals: The key divergence worth flagging is SPX/HYG: HYG is -0.23% over 5 days while equities rallied — the 30D correlation between SPX and HYG is 0.88, so credit diverging is an early warning. If HYG continues to lag, it typically precedes equity stress by 1-2 weeks. Additionally, Gold's 30D correlation with DXY is -0.28 (weak negative), suggesting gold is not yet in a pure dollar-driven move — instead, the correction appears to be position-driven after the blow-off top near $5,318.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C at 1.35 and QQQ P/C at 1.31 both indicate elevated put buying. This is a bearish lean from the options market, despite the equity price action remaining near highs. This hedging activity could provide a short-term floor on selloffs (gamma support) but also caps upside enthusiasm.

Sector Rotation: Technology (XLK) is the clear institutional favorite — +18.47% over 1 month, +9.57% relative to SPX. The mega-cap earnings beat cycle (AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, GOOGL, META all beat Q1) is driving concentrated capital flows into tech. Energy (XLE) is paradoxically *underperforming* despite WTI at $105 — only +2.91% over 5 days vs. XLK's +2.66%, and -0.49% 1-month vs. SPX's +8.91%. This E&P/Oil divergence (oil up 83% YTD, XLE lagging) suggests the market is pricing in either demand destruction from a slowing economy or near-term peak oil. Healthcare (XLV) is the weakest sector 1-month (-9.97% relative to SPX) — watch for rotation into value names here.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — Nonfarm Payrolls (May 8, 08:30 ET):

The April jobs report is the week's defining print. Market consensus is tracking for ~175,000–200,000 new jobs, with unemployment holding near 4.2%. The asymmetric risk scenario is critical to understand:

- Beat scenario (>220K): Reinforces "no landing" — equities initially spike but yields surge toward the 4.60% 52W high, DXY bounces, and gold gets a second leg lower. The FOMC's dissenter hawks (Hammack, Kashkari, Logan) gain ammunition against the easing bias, pressuring rate-sensitive equities (XLRE, XLU). Net read: initially bullish, then hawkish headwind within 48 hours.

- Miss scenario (<150K): Markets interpret as demand destruction from energy shock. Equities sell off, yields fall, gold recovers sharply. The easing bias gets vindicated — futures re-price a July cut. This is the "stagflation confirmation" path where nothing works except gold, short bonds, and cash.

- In-line scenario: Consolidation. Tech holds, oil is the wildcard. Watch Iran headlines over the weekend.

The jobs print will be *interpreted through the oil lens* — a strong number with WTI at $105 means the Fed is stuck, full stop.
