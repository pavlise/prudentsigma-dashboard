uncertainty. Today's session hinges on PPI data (the inflation sequel to
last week's hot CPI print) and the formal opening of Q1 2026 bank earnings
season, which collectively will set the Fed narrative heading into the
April 28-29 FOMC meeting.

SECTION 2 — TECHNICAL SNAPSHOT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Equities are constructively bullish — SPX and NDX sit
  above their SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200, a clean triple-stack bull structure.
  In sharp contrast, DXY trades below all three moving averages simultaneously,
  a structural bear configuration that is the single most important technical
  fact in markets right now, as dollar weakness is the lubricant for this
  entire risk rally. WTI is the outlier: above SMA50/200 (war-premium
  residue) but slipping below SMA20, suggesting supply return is beginning
  to tip the balance.

- Momentum: RSI readings cluster in the 52-66 range across assets —
  neither overbought nor oversold. The most notable divergence: **Copper's
  RSI at 66 is the highest in the dashboard**, consistent with its Bollinger
  Band reading of 1.05 (above the upper band — a breakout signal requiring
  confirmation). Gold and Silver both sit at RSI 52 — momentum is moderate
  and not stretched, which is constructive for further upside if the dollar
  continues weakening. BTC's RSI at 62 with a bullish MACD is encouraging
  but hemmed in by the SMA200 bear wall at $87,523.

- Volatility: ATR% tells a story of bifurcation. Equities (SPX ATR 1.43%,
  NDX 1.74%) have normalised. WTI at 8.66% ATR remains extremely volatile
  — a single geopolitical headline moves it several percent. Silver's ATR of
  5.12% offers a high-volatility leverage play on the metal complex. VIX
  term structure is in contango (VIX 19.12 vs. VIX3M 21.34), which reflects
  a market pricing in elevated uncertainty beyond the near-term — not pure
  complacency.

- Cross-asset signals: The key correlation to watch is **WTI/SPX at
  -0.57**. This negative relationship has been the regime-defining trade
  of 2026: when oil spikes on geopolitical fear, equities fall, and vice
  versa. With oil softening post-ceasefire, equities benefit mechanically.
  Separately, HYG has rallied +0.68% over 5 days and remains tightly
  correlated to SPX (0.88), confirming credit is not flashing distress —
  the equity rally has credit support. The Gold/DXY near-zero correlation
  (−0.08) suggests gold is now driven by central bank demand and safe-haven
  flows more than pure dollar moves — a bullish structural shift.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C ratio of 1.33 signals **elevated put
  buying** — the market is hedged, not euphoric. This is a contrarian
  bullish signal; persistent put demand acts as gamma fuel for further
  upside squeezes as market makers delta-hedge. QQQ P/C at 1.19 is
  neutral. The asymmetry favors upside surprises if PPI prints benign.

Sector Rotation: Technology (XLK +5.95% 5D, +1.88% relative) and
Industrials (XLI +5.14%, +1.07% relative) are this week's unambiguous
leaders — a classic cyclical risk-on rotation. Energy (XLE −5.07% 5D,
−9.14% relative) is the week's catastrophic laggard as oil sheds its
war premium. Defensives (XLP, XLU) are also underperforming, consistent
with genuine risk appetite rather than a defensive rotation. Financials
(XLF) tracking roughly in line with SPX, with bank earnings beginning
today a critical test of whether the sector can sustain its +6.21% 1M
gain. The read: institutional money is rotating INTO cyclicals/tech and
OUT of energy/defensives — a textbook early-expansion playbook.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — PPI (April 14, Today):
This is the most consequential data point of the week. Following a hotter-
than-expected March CPI, all eyes are on wholesale prices to assess whether
inflation is re-accelerating or was a one-time tariff-driven distortion.
The Fed is holding rates at 3.50–3.75% with PCE at 2.7% — above the 2%
target. A hot PPI print (consensus: +0.3% M/M core) would:
(1) push 10Y yields toward 4.40-4.50%, (2) compress equity valuations,
(3) pressure gold/silver near-term. A cool or in-line print would be
the green light for the current risk-on momentum to extend toward SPX
6,978 (52W high). The asymmetry is clear: a miss to the upside is a more
violent catalyst than a beat, given current positioning (elevated put
hedges but SPY near upper BB). FOMC on April 28-29 is the next binary
event; Fed is universally expected to hold, but the dot plot guidance
will be scrutinised for signs of a July cut.
