Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The global macro backdrop is being rewritten by a single, dominant force: the
Middle East energy shock. "Operation Epic Fury" — the US-Israeli strike on
Iranian strategic infrastructure in late February — has effectively closed the
Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping, stranding an estimated 20 million
barrels/day and catalyzing WTI's historic +95% YTD surge. A reported 48-hour
deadline over further offensive action against Iran sets the stage for a
kinetic open today. Markets are no longer trading macro fundamentals; they are
trading geopolitical optionality, with every Hormuz headline a volatility
trigger. This week's CPI print (Friday) — the first to capture full oil pass-
through at the consumer level — represents the single most important data
event of Q2 2026.

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Broadly bearish for equities. SPX and NDX sit below
  their SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200 — a textbook bear alignment. The SMA200 at
  6,644 (SPX) is the immediate reclaim level; failure to close above it this
  week confirms that the 5-day bounce (+3.36%) was a dead-cat correction, not
  a trend reversal. WTI is the lone clean bull: above all three SMAs with the
  SMA50 at 78.58 far below current price of 111.94 — the trend structure is
  parabolic.

- Momentum: RSI readings show divergence across asset classes. WTI is the
  only overbought asset (RSI 71) — a pullback risk is real but supply-driven
  bids will likely support any dip. SPX (RSI 46) and NDX (RSI 47) are neutral,
  not oversold — there is no capitulation signal yet, which argues the 5-day
  bounce lacks conviction and a re-test of lows is plausible. Gold (RSI 47)
  and Silver (RSI 44) are both mid-range, suggesting selling pressure has
  washed out but a catalytic bid is needed for resumption. BTC/ETH RSIs (51/53)
  are neutral with mild bullish lean.

- Volatility: VIX at 23.87 in CONTANGO (VIX3M at 24.72) signals the
  market is pricing in sustained uncertainty over 3 months — not a spike-and-
  resolve scenario. ATR% is notably elevated in WTI (7.28%) and Silver (6.41%),
  making position sizing critical. Gold ATR at 3.22% remains manageable for
  tactical positioning. SPX ATR of 1.61% suggests the index is still in active
  discovery, not a quiet grind.

- Cross-asset signals: The DXY/Gold correlation has broken down (30D: -0.08,
  near zero). Gold is no longer being driven by dollar dynamics — it is in pure
  geopolitical safe-haven mode. This is structurally significant: it means
  dollar strength alone cannot suppress gold. SPX/HYG correlation at 0.87
  remains high — watch HYG (currently +1.56% 5D) closely; if credit diverges
  from equities, that's an early warning of equity stress. WTI/DXY correlation
  (+0.59) confirms oil is bidding the dollar alongside geopolitical risk premium.

- Options sentiment: SPY put/call ratio at 1.55 and QQQ at 1.60 signal
  elevated institutional hedging. This is not panic-level (which would be >2.0)
  but confirms smart money is not "all in" on the equity bounce. The put-heavy
  skew is a structural ceiling on any rally without a genuine geopolitical
  de-escalation catalyst.

Sector Rotation: Energy (XLE, +5.59% 1M vs SPX -3.63%) is the clear
institutional destination. Tech (XLK, +4.67% 5D) is leading the short-term
bounce but still -2.87% 1M. Utilities (XLU, +3.13% 1M relative) are quietly
outperforming — a defensive accumulation signal. Industrials (-4.55% 1M) and
Healthcare (-4.22% 1M) are the laggards, suggesting the rotation is away from
cyclical growth and into stagflation hedges (Energy + defensive yield plays).
The message is clear: institutions are not buying a growth revival; they are
repositioning for a commodity-driven inflation regime.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — Friday, April 10: March CPI

This is the most consequential data print of Q2 2026. March CPI will be the
first official readout to capture the consumer-level pass-through of WTI's
+23% monthly surge. Energy is the largest single-month contributor to CPI
in recent memory, and headline inflation is expected to print materially above
consensus. The Fed is caught in a classic stagflationary bind: inflation is
being driven by a supply shock (Hormuz closure), not demand — rate hikes
cannot fix it, but the Fed cannot ignore a hot print with labor markets still
firm.

Asymmetric Scenarios:
- Bear case (hot CPI >+0.6% MoM): Yields spike above 4.45%, dollar surges,
  equities re-test the 6,343 SMA20-range low. Gold/Silver initially sold as
  real yield pressure rises, then bid as stagflation narrative dominates.
- Bull case (CPI in-line or soft): SPX reclaims SMA200 at 6,644, VIX
  compresses toward 20, risk-on rotation into tech. Unlikely given oil data.
- Wildcard: Hormuz de-escalation over the weekend would crash WTI, collapse
  energy CPI expectations, and trigger a sharp equity rally. Monitor all
  diplomatic channels closely.
