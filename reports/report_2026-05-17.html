HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — FOMC Minutes (Wednesday, May 20):
This is not a routine minutes release. The FOMC meeting captured here was
Jerome Powell's final policy act as Chair, with rates held at 3.50–3.75% amid
a historically large number of dissenting votes — a rare, market-moving signal
of deep internal disagreement. Incoming Chair Kevin Warsh is widely perceived
as hawkish, and markets will parse every dissenting argument for signals of
whether the new leadership will pivot to hikes.

*Base case:* Minutes reveal 3–4 dissents favoring rate hikes, confirming
hawkish shift. 10Y yields test 4.60–4.70%, tech sells off, DXY extends.

*Bull scenario:* Language is more balanced than feared; "data dependent" framing
dominates. Yields pull back to 4.40%, QQQ bounces from oversold tech.

*Bear scenario:* Warsh allies explicitly telegraphed a hike path. Markets price
a June hike — yields spike toward 4.80%, equities break SMA20 support, VIX
pushes through 20.

Asymmetric risk skews bearish: options market is already pricing elevated
post-minutes vol (QQQ P/C volume 1.40), and the data regime (hot CPI, oil
above $100) provides cover for a hawkish surprise.
