Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Bullish across equities — SPX and NDX are above SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200 simultaneously. Gold is in a corrective phase (below SMA20/50) but structural bull (SMA200 +8.4%). DXY is below all three MAs — a confirmed bearish trend providing a macro tailwind for risk assets and commodities priced in USD. WTI's structure is the most aggressive: +46.9% above SMA200, a parabolic regime that rarely sustains without catalyst.

- Momentum: The equity complex is unambiguously overbought. NDX RSI 75 and SPX RSI 71 are in territory historically associated with 5-10% mean-reversion drawdowns within 4-6 weeks. Gold at RSI 44 and Silver at RSI 50 are neutral-to-oversold — constructive for accumulation. BTC at RSI 66 has room to run before hitting overbought; ETH at RSI 60 is lagging BTC but recovering.

- Volatility: VIX at 16.99 with a contango term structure (VIX3M at 20.37) signals the market is pricing in near-term calm but medium-term uncertainty — a classic "sell the vol spike, buy the dip" environment. ATR% on WTI (7.22%) and Silver (3.78%) signal high intraday ranges; position sizing must account for this. SPX ATR at 1.07% is benign.

- Cross-asset signals: The SPX/HYG correlation of +0.88 confirms credit is supporting the equity rally — no dislocation signal yet. The unusual WTI/SPX correlation of -0.63 means oil's +77% YTD surge has been a headwind that equities have shrugged off; a sharp oil decline (ceasefire scenario) could actually be *equity positive*. Gold/DXY at -0.28 is weaker than historical norms — Gold is finding a floor from safe-haven demand independent of dollar moves.

- Options sentiment: QQQ put/call at 1.60 reflects elevated hedging in tech-heavy positions — smart money is buying downside insurance even as the index hits highs. SPY at 1.18 is near neutral. This divergence is a yellow flag for NDX specifically.

Sector Rotation: Technology (XLK +19.03% 1M, +9.20% vs SPX) is in a class of its own — institutions are loading AI-exposed names aggressively. Energy (XLE -10.51% vs SPX 1M) and Healthcare (XLV -10.96% vs SPX 1M) are the significant underperformers. Defensive rotation (Staples, Utilities) has lagged, confirming a genuine risk-on bid rather than defensive positioning. Real Estate (XLRE +6.51% 1M) is quietly outperforming — likely benefiting from the narrative of eventual Fed cuts stabilizing mortgage rates.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — FOMC Rate Decision (May 6):
The Fed is widely expected to hold rates unchanged with Bloomberg Economics estimating April CPI around 3.4% YoY — well above the 2% target. The key asymmetric risk lies in the *tone* of Powell's press conference, not the decision itself. A hawkish hold (emphasis on tariff-driven inflation persistence and no cuts this year) could pressure long-duration assets (TLT, NDX, Gold) significantly — recall the 10Y yield is already 4.38% and approaching its 4.60% 52W high. A dovish hold (acknowledging labor market softening and signaling eventual flexibility) would be the green light for a second leg in the equity rally. Current futures pricing suggests roughly a 33% probability of even one cut in 2026 — any language shift around this probability is the market mover. Position asymmetry: short-dated vol in TLT and NDX options looks cheap heading into Wednesday.

Additional Watch: A Trump-Xi summit reportedly rescheduled for mid-May could be the next macro shock — a trade deal announcement would be unambiguously risk-on (equities +, dollar -, gold -, commodities +).
