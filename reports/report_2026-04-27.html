HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT: Q1 2026 GDP Advance + PCE — Thursday April 30

This is the week's asymmetric risk pivot. The Atlanta Fed GDPNow sits at
1.2% SAAR — a shock slowdown — while the NY Fed's model reads 2.4%.
This 120bp dispersion is unusually wide and reflects genuine uncertainty
around tariff-driven inventory distortions and soft retail sales.

Bull scenario (GDP ≥ 2.0% + Core PCE ≤ 2.6%): Risk assets rally
strongly — confirms soft landing, pressure off Fed, DXY weakens further,
equities and gold bid simultaneously.

Bear scenario (GDP < 1.5% + Core PCE > 2.8%): Stagflation read.
Bond market conflict: yields fall on growth fear but Fed hands are tied
by sticky inflation. Equities reprice lower sharply; dollar could catch
a safe-haven bid despite technical bear structure; VIX spikes above 22.

The Hormuz oil spike complicates PCE directly — energy pass-through into
March CPI/PCE is real with WTI at $96+. Markets may not be positioned
for a stagflation print. This makes Thursday the most asymmetric risk
event of the week, not the FOMC (where a hold is 100% priced).
