dissent, mega-cap tech drove the S&P 500 to a fresh all-time high, and options
markets are quietly loading up on protection — a divergence worth watching closely.

SECTION 2 — TECHNICAL SNAPSHOT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend Structure: US equities are in a textbook bull structure: SPX and NDX
  are above their SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200. However, the bull run has an expiry
  date problem — NDX's 15.75% one-month surge has pushed it 12.6% above its
  SMA200, a historically extended condition. WTI is similarly stretched (+44.3%
  above SMA200). Gold and Silver are in mixed structure: below their SMA20/SMA50
  after pulling back from all-time highs, but firmly above SMA200 (+9.1% and
  +22.3% respectively), defining the longer-term uptrend as intact.

- Momentum: The RSI picture is bifurcated. SPX at RSI 71 and NDX at RSI 76
  are both in overbought territory — NDX in particular is flashing late-stage
  momentum exhaustion. Gold (RSI 48) and Silver (RSI 51) have reset to neutral
  after their prior run, creating a healthier re-entry setup. WTI (RSI 53) is
  mid-range despite its massive YTD gain, suggesting the energy move has
  institutional breadth rather than purely speculative froth. BTC's RSI of 65
  is constructive but faces the SMA200 wall at $83,544.

- Volatility: VIX at 17.38 is low in absolute terms, but the contango term
  structure (VIX 17.38 vs. VIX3M 20.82 — a 20% premium at the 3-month tenor)
  tells a different story: the market is calm today but pricing in elevated
  uncertainty ahead. WTI's ATR% of 7.22% is the highest across assets — crude
  is the volatility epicenter. Gold's ATR% of 2.01% and BTC's 2.68% reflect
  elevated but manageable risk in those assets.

- Cross-Asset Signals: The most anomalous correlation is Gold/SPX at +0.45
  (30-day rolling). Gold typically rallies in risk-off regimes, yet it is moving
  in tandem with equities — signaling that the gold bid is driven by structural
  inflation and USD debasement fears (Iran war premium + energy inflation), not
  pure flight-to-safety. The SPX/DXY correlation of -0.70 supports this: dollar
  weakness is the common engine lifting both equities (via earnings translation)
  and commodities. Credit (HYG +0.26% 5D, LQD +0.32% 5D) is holding well and
  confirming the equity rally — no credit stress signal yet.

- Options Sentiment: Both SPY (P/C 1.42) and QQQ (P/C 1.66) show elevated
  put buying relative to calls. This is NOT a panic signal at current VIX levels
  — it is systematic hedging at all-time highs. It implies institutional investors
  are bullish enough to hold positions but cautious enough to pay for downside
  protection. The net read: rally continuation is possible but narrow and fragile.

Sector Rotation: Technology (XLK) is the unambiguous leader — +20.52% in
one month, +10.81% relative to SPX — driven by mega-cap earnings beats and AI
capex commitments from MSFT, META, and GOOGL. Remarkably, Energy (XLE) is
lagging SPX by -10.89% over the same month despite WTI +6.27%, suggesting the
market is treating high crude as a *headwind tax* on the broader economy rather
than an energy sector profit story. Healthcare (XLV, -10.57% relative) continues
its structural underperformance. The rotation thesis: AI/tech momentum > commodity
producers; risk appetite is concentrated and narrowing.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — US CPI (April), expected ~May 12:
The April CPI print will be the week's most consequential release for risk assets.
March CPI came in at 3.3% YoY (core 2.6%), already well above the Fed's 2% target.
With WTI averaging near $95–$105 through April and gasoline prices rising at the
pump, April headline CPI could print at 3.5–3.8% — a new cycle high. The asymmetric
risk here is clearly to the upside: a hot print re-prices rate cut expectations to
zero for 2026 (already largely discounted per Fed funds futures, but a 3.8%+ print
could trigger a further hawkish repricing). The equity market is most vulnerable
at current overbought RSI levels if rates reverse sharply. Conversely, a cooler
core read (ex-energy) preserves the disinflation narrative and supports the rally.
Position sizing should be reduced ahead of this print.
