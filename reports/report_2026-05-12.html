HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — April CPI (Today, 08:30 ET):

This is the most consequential data release in weeks. With headline CPI expected
at +3.7% YoY (vs. +3.3% prior), markets are already pricing a hot number —
but the asymmetry is extreme. An upside surprise above +4.0% would crater rate-
cut expectations entirely, trigger a DXY spike, hit gold, and potentially
send NDX (RSI 83) into a sharp technical correction of 5-8%. The Fed is already
paralyzed: energy-driven inflation constrains easing, but the economy needs
relief as the Hormuz-driven supply shock bites into demand.

Bull scenario (CPI ≤ 3.5%): Core comes in soft, energy stripped out reveals
underlying disinflation. Risk-on continues; NDX breaks above Bollinger Band
expansion. Gold stays bid. Rate-cut odds for H2 2026 improve.

Bear scenario (CPI ≥ 4.0%): The market is forced to reprice the "no cut"
scenario well into 2027 under an uncertain new Fed Chair. 10Y yield re-tests
4.60% resistance. SPX gives up SMA20 at 7,180. Dollar catches a short squeeze.

Additionally, Friday's Fed Chair transition (Powell's term expires May 15)
is deeply underappreciated by markets. Kevin Warsh's expected confirmation as
Chair carries hawkish risk — Warsh has historically been more aggressive on
inflation control, which could reprice the policy path materially.
