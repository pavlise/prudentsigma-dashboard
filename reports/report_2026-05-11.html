These two dynamics are in tension: diplomatic progress is risk-on, but sticky inflation is a rate-regime spoiler.

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Unambiguously bullish on equities. SPX sits 9.6% above its SMA200 (6,753) and 7.8% above SMA50 (6,863). NDX is an extraordinary 17.1% above its SMA200 (24,956). The April rally — SPX's best month since November 2020 — has not mean-reverted; price is making new 52-week highs. The only bear structure in the core assets is BTC, which remains 2.3% below its SMA200 at $82,579 — a key ceiling to watch for a regime flip.

- Momentum: NDX RSI at 83 is the most critical red flag in today's dashboard — this is not merely "overbought," it is historically extended and statistically prone to compression. SPX RSI at 75 confirms participation is broad, but not catastrophic. Silver's RSI at 59 with a bullish MACD is textbook — strong momentum without the froth of equities, offering better risk/reward on the long side. Gold at RSI 50 and WTI at 52 are neutral, with directional bias coming from macro rather than technicals.

- Volatility: VIX at 18.22 in contango (VIX3M at 20.50) is normal structure — no systemic panic is priced. However, VIX is +25.57% YTD, a persistent risk-premium bid that tells us institutional hedges remain in place. NDX ATR% at 1.44% vs SPX at 1.04% highlights that tech is carrying disproportionate intraday risk. Silver's ATR% at 3.80% is the most live asset for active traders. WTI's 7.66% ATR is extreme — crude is a volatility trap.

- Cross-asset signals: The DXY (97.97) is below all three SMAs and in a bearish MACD regime — this is a meaningful structural dollar weakening. This is a direct tailwind for Gold, Silver, and commodities priced in USD. Critically, the DXY/Gold correlation (-0.58 over 30D) confirms that gold's current consolidation is being partly suppressed by dollar resilience from oil-driven inflation; a DXY breakdown below 97.84 would likely re-ignite the precious metals bid. HYG at 80.14 is performing — no credit stress signal; the SPX/HYG correlation of +0.86 confirms credit and equity are in sync, validating the risk-on regime.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C at 1.15 is neutral, but QQQ P/C at 1.48 signals elevated put-buying in tech — the options market is hedging the overbought NDX, not chasing it. This is consistent with smart money locking in gains on the tech rally while maintaining index exposure.

Sector Rotation: Tech (XLK) is the overwhelming 1-month leader at +23.07%, a massive +14.53% outperformance vs SPX — AI earnings revisions and US-China diplomatic thaw are the drivers. Energy (XLE) is the laggard at -10.72% relative to SPX in 1M, as WTI pulls back from 52W highs. Healthcare (XLV) and Utilities (XLU) are the defensive underperformers (-11.1% and -13.3% relative), confirming this is a risk-on, growth-seeking rotation — not a defensive bid. Financials' -7.6% relative underperformance despite JPM and GS blowout earnings suggests valuation concerns over the banking sector in a higher-for-longer rate regime.

This is the single most market-moving data point of the week, arriving in a regime where the Fed has already paused and inflation persistence is the primary threat to the bull case. Consensus: headline +3.7% YoY (MoM +0.6%), core +2.7% YoY (MoM +0.3%). The asymmetric risk is to the upside: WTI crude has surged +72.66% YTD and Brent is near $120 — energy components will mechanically boost headline. Services inflation remains sticky. A print above +3.9% headline or +2.9% core would force a repricing of Fed expectations toward "higher for longer" and potentially re-ignite the 10Y yield toward its 52W high of 4.60%, pressuring the overbought NDX (RSI 83) into a sharp correction. Conversely, an in-line or softer print (≤3.6% headline) would be the green light for SPX to challenge new highs and confirm the bull trend. Positioning: the QQQ put/call ratio of 1.48 suggests the market is already partially hedged for downside in tech — a soft CPI would trigger a violent short-squeeze in NDX.
