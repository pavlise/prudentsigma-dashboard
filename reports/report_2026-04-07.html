SECTION 2 — TECHNICAL SNAPSHOT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend Structure: SPX sits in a structurally damaged zone — above SMA20
  (6,601) but below both SMA50 (6,777) and SMA200 (6,647). The SMA50 > SMA200
  death-cross is forming; sustained closes below SMA200 would confirm a
  medium-term bear structure. NDX is below all three MAs, already in bear
  alignment. The exception is WTI, which is emphatically bull — 17.9% above
  SMA20, 44.6% above SMA50, 73.6% above SMA200.

- Momentum: RSI readings are broadly neutral across risk assets (SPX 48,
  NDX 49, Gold 47, Silver 45, BTC 50) — no asset is deeply oversold to create
  a clean mean-reversion entry. The critical outlier is WTI at RSI 73
  (overbought) with BB% at 1.03 (above the upper Bollinger Band) — a textbook
  exhaustion signal that typically precedes a 5-15% correction once the catalyst
  resolves. MACD is Bullish across most assets (SPX, NDX, Gold, Silver, BTC,
  WTI) — momentum breadth is constructive, but trend structure overrules.

- Volatility: VIX at 24.17 has collapsed -21% in 5 days (from ~30), with
  the term structure in contango (VIX3M 24.77 > VIX 24.17) — a "normal" fear
  gradient, not the extreme backwardation seen during shock events. WTI ATR%
  at 6.86% and Silver ATR% at 6.00% signal high realized vol in commodity
  space. SPX ATR% 1.53% is elevated relative to its pre-2026 norm.

- Cross-Asset Signals: The Gold/DXY 30-day correlation is -0.08 (nearly
  zero vs. the typical -0.30 to -0.50 range). Gold is NOT being driven by
  dollar weakness — it is a pure safe-haven flight. The WTI/VIX correlation
  of +0.51 is unusual: oil and fear rising together signals a supply shock
  premium, not a demand boom. SPX/HYG at +0.87 means credit markets are not
  sounding alarm bells yet — no credit dislocation beneath the equity surface.
  Watch for SPX/HYG divergence as the first warning of systemic stress.

- Options Sentiment: SPY P/C at 1.31 and QQQ P/C at 1.56 are in elevated
  put-buying territory — institutions are hedged. This has a contrarian
  interpretation: heavy put positioning can create a short-squeeze rally if
  the geopolitical risk premium deflates quickly on a ceasefire announcement.

Sector Rotation: XLK (Tech) is the standout leader at +7.28% 5D vs. SPX
+4.23%, suggesting rotation into high-duration growth on ceasefire optimism and
falling rate expectations (10Y yield down 0.16% over 5 days). Energy (XLE) is
paradoxically lagging at -3.68% 5D despite WTI at $115 — the market is pricing
in margin compression from input costs and a looming oil reversal, not energy
sector upside. Defensives (XLP, XLV) are the biggest relative laggards,
confirming this week's rally is selective risk-on, not defensive rotation.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — Friday April 10: US CPI (March)

This is the most consequential data print of the quarter. March CPI will be the
first reading to capture WTI crude's +21.58% surge during the month. Energy
will mechanically push headline CPI well above expectations — consensus sits
near +0.4% MoM headline. A +0.5%+ print would force markets to reprice Fed
rate cuts entirely out of 2026, driving 10Y yields back toward 4.50-4.60%
and pressuring growth multiples. The asymmetric risk is to the upside: if
crude pulls back on a ceasefire, energy's CPI contribution softens — but
services and shelter remain sticky. Core CPI (ex-food/energy) is the critical
component to watch. A core miss could paradoxically be bullish for equities
(Fed stays accommodative) even with hot headline.

Tonight's Iran Deadline (Binary Risk Event):
Ceasefire → WTI -10 to -15%, SPX +1.5-2%, VIX → 18-20, Gold softens.
No Deal / Escalation → WTI +8-12%, SPX -2 to -3%, VIX → 32-38, Gold +3%.
