Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis remains the single most powerful force reshaping global markets. With the strait practically closed — choking off ~35% of seaborne crude — WTI has surged +83.5% YTD and the World Bank now projects energy prices to rise 24% this year, the sharpest shock since 2022. This stagflationary vortex (surging energy, slowing industrial output, elevated inflation) is colliding directly with a FOMC meeting next week (May 6–7), where Chair Powell — whose term expires May 15 — faces an impossible dilemma: inflation running hot at ~3.5% YoY while global growth signals are deteriorating. Markets are grinding to all-time highs on equity momentum, but the macro underpinning is fragile.

Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: The bull structure in equities is unambiguous — SPX and NDX are above all three major moving averages (SMA20/50/200), with SPX +7.2% above its SMA200 and NDX +10.7% above its own. WTI is even more extreme at +52.4% above SMA200. In contrast, Gold and Silver are below their SMA20 and SMA50 (in near-term correction mode), while BTC is below its SMA200 ($84,208) — a structural bear signal for crypto. DXY is in full bear structure: below SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200.

- Momentum: The critical divergence here is in precious metals. Gold RSI (44) and Silver RSI (47) are approaching the oversold zone despite both being well above their SMA200s — this suggests the recent pullback is a technical cooling, not a structural reversal. SPX and NDX at RSI 70/73 are flashing caution; these levels have historically preceded 3–5% consolidations. WTI RSI (60) remains healthy — not overbought — implying the energy trend has more runway.

- Volatility: VIX at 16.89 in contango (VIX3M = 20.08) signals that the market is pricing near-term calm but hedging further out. ATR% on WTI (6.86%) and Silver (3.91%) are the highest in the matrix — these are the highest-volatility trades available. SPX ATR% of 1.09% means a typical daily swing of ~$79 points. The BB%B reading of 0.79 on SPX and 0.81 on NDX confirms prices are pressing the upper Bollinger Band — mean reversion risk is elevated.

- Cross-asset signals: Two notable divergences: (1) Gold/DXY correlation is near zero (-0.01) — historically gold falls with DXY, but both are declining together, suggesting gold's pullback is purely technical profit-taking, not dollar-driven; this is bullish for the next gold leg. (2) SPX/HYG correlation at +0.86 is elevated — HYG (high yield) is only -0.12% over 5 days vs SPX +0.61%, a small divergence that should be monitored; credit stress precedes equity weakness historically.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C at 1.17 is neutral; QQQ P/C at 1.53 is meaningfully elevated — institutional players are buying puts on tech. This is consistent with the NDX being at overbought RSI 73 ahead of the FOMC. Smart money is hedging, not exiting.

Sector Rotation: Tech (XLK) is the dominant 1-month leader at +18.23% vs SPX's +9.64% — a massive +8.59% relative outperformance. Energy (XLE) leads the 5-day rotation (+4.89% raw, +4.28% relative) as the Hormuz crisis reprices crude. Healthcare (XLV) is the laggard at -10.82% relative to SPX over the month. The rotation story: momentum money is chasing AI-driven tech (post-earnings beats from NVDA, META, MSFT) while simultaneously the oil shock is forcing tactical rotation into energy. Defensives (XLP, XLU) are underperforming, confirming residual risk-on behavior — but this could flip sharply if FOMC disappoints.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — FOMC May 7, 2026:

The market consensus is a hold at 3.75%, but the *language* is what matters. With WTI +83% YTD and CPI running ~3.5% YoY from the Hormuz oil shock, Powell has no room to signal rate cuts. The asymmetric risk is hawkish: if Powell explicitly pushes back on rate cut pricing or raises the inflation risk assessment, risk assets will reprice sharply lower from overbought levels. The additional wrinkle is Powell's term expiring May 15 — any political noise around his successor could add to uncertainty. If, on the other hand, the Fed signals that the oil shock is "transitory" and growth concerns dominate, we could see a brief equity melt-up. Positioning: SPX/NDX at RSI 70+ means the *ceiling* for a relief rally is limited, but the *floor* on a hawkish shock could be -4% to -6%. The risk/reward for being long equities into the FOMC announcement is unfavorable.
