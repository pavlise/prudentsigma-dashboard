Technical Condition Summary:

- Trend structure: Structurally bullish for equities. SPX and NDX are above their SMA20, SMA50, and SMA200, a full-stack bull configuration. The 1-month rally (+12.5% SPX, +17.8% NDX) has been powerful — but both are now within 1–2% of 52W highs, limiting near-term upside without a fresh catalyst. WTI mirrors this — above all three MAs — though its MACD is turning bearish, a divergence worth watching.

- Momentum: NDX RSI at 69 and SPX at 67 are technically not overbought (threshold 70), but are in the "extended" zone where pullbacks become more likely. Notably, Gold's RSI has compressed to 42 and Silver to 46 — both approaching oversold territory relative to their recent highs — while remaining above SMA200 (+8.8% and +20.8% respectively). This is a classic mid-correction dip in a secular bull trend for precious metals.

- Volatility: VIX at 17.83 (down from 31 just six weeks ago) represents a sharp complacency reset. The VIX term structure is in contango — VIX3M at 20.49 vs. spot 17.83 — signaling that options markets are hedging tail risk further out, not today. ATR% is highest in WTI (7.14%), Silver (3.86%), and ETH (3.87%) — the most volatile instruments in today's universe. SPX ATR at 1.13% is calm, but that belies the event risk embedded in tonight's schedule.

- Cross-asset signals: The Gold/DXY 30-day correlation is +0.04 — effectively zero. This is a critical read: Gold is no longer moving on dollar dynamics. It is a pure war-safe-haven bid. When this correlation turns negative again (dollar weakens AND gold rallies), it historically signals the next leg higher in precious metals. HYG/SPX at +0.86 confirms the credit market is still validating equities — no credit stress emerging. WTI/SPX at -0.61 is the key headwind: rising oil is a tax on corporate margins and consumer spending, and historically dampens equity multiples.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C ratio at 1.17 and QQQ at 1.06 — both tilting mildly bearish/neutral. Markets are buying protective puts into the event cluster, not capitulating. This is healthy hedging, not panic.

Sector Rotation: Technology (XLK +23.8% vs. SPX +12.5% over 1M) is the dominant flow story — institutions are buying the dip in AI infrastructure names despite yesterday's OpenAI concerns. Real Estate (XLRE +9.2%) and Industrials (XLI +9.2%) are secondary leaders, suggesting rate-stabilization hopes. The critical laggard is Energy (XLE -6.86% 1M relative) — despite WTI at $99, the sector is being sold. This disconnect signals that investors view the oil spike as a conflict-driven, temporary premium rather than a durable demand story. Healthcare (XLV nearly flat 1M) and Staples (XLP +1.5% 1M) both lag sharply — classic risk-on rotation away from defensives.
