building ahead of this binary risk event.

  WTI RSI 53 is neutral, suggesting the energy move still has legs before

- Volatility: VIX at 19.31 has risen +33% YTD — no longer complacent.
  The VIX term structure is in contango (VIX3M 21.48 > VIX 19.31),
  signaling the market is pricing in more uncertainty ahead, not an
  immediate spike. WTI's ATR% of 8.03% screams crude volatility; Silver

  Bollinger Bands are near upper rails: SPX BB%B 0.79, NDX 0.82 — sellers
  historically dominate at these levels unless a fresh catalyst sustains
  momentum.

  zero (-0.01) — a structural anomaly. Gold is not trading as a dollar
  hedge today; it is a pure geopolitical/war premium asset. DXY MACD is
  bearish while EUR/USD is in a confirmed bull trend (above all SMAs) —
  dollar structural weakness persists. SPX/HYG correlation at 0.87 means
  credit is not flashing stress; this is constructive for equities.
  SPX/WTI at -0.64 is the key macro tension: every $10 rise in oil
  historically shaves ~0.5% off S&P EPS.

- Options sentiment: SPY P/C at 1.43 and QQQ P/C at 1.81 are
  materially elevated — institutional desks are buying puts aggressively
  ahead of April 29. This is not panic; it is hedging. The contrarian
  read: if FOMC+earnings deliver positive surprises, put-covering could
  amplify any upside move sharply (gamma squeeze risk).

Sector Rotation: Tech (XLK) is the undisputed leader: +13.95% over
one month, +6.12% vs SPX. This is consistent with earnings beats from
MSFT, GOOGL, META, NVDA (recent) and pre-earnings positioning. Real
Estate (XLRE) is quietly outperforming (+1.33% relative 1M) — a signal

glaring anomaly: despite WTI at $96, XLE is -13.76% relative to SPX over
one month — the market is discounting energy equities on concerns about
reserves releases, potential Iran ceasefire, and margin compression from
upstream costs. Healthcare (XLV) -7.84% relative signals defensive
rotation is not in play — risk appetite remains active.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — April 29: FOMC + Mega-Cap Earnings Convergence

This is a once-in-a-cycle confluence. The Fed has maintained rates steady
as it navigates the dual shock of elevated oil prices (inflation risk) and
tariff-related growth headwinds (recession risk). Market consensus expects
a hold, but the tone of Powell's press conference — particularly his
reaction function to the Iran-driven energy inflation and whether he
signals a June cut window — will be the defining catalyst.

*Earnings asymmetry:* MSFT, GOOGL, META, and AMZN together represent
~22% of NDX. Analyst targets for MSFT ($578, +39% upside) and META ($855,
+30%) suggest the Street is constructive. Recent beats from MSFT Q2 FY26
($4.14 vs $3.92 est) and META Q4 2025 ($8.88 vs $8.22) set a high bar.
Bull scenario: FOMC neutral-to-dovish + earnings beats + guidance
raises → NDX punches through 26,937 all-time high, put-covering amplifies
the move. Bear scenario: FOMC hawkish (oil/inflation concern) + any
guidance disappointment from AMZN/GOOGL → NDX unwinding from RSI 71
overextension, potential -4 to -6% drawdown in days. The asymmetry favors
positioning for volatility itself rather than directional betting.
