────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Dominant Macro Theme Today:
The global macro story is being written by one event: the Strait of Hormuz
blockade, triggered by "Operation Epic Fury" in late February 2026, which has
effectively cut off ~20% of global oil flows. WTI crude has surged +95.5% YTD
to $112.06, creating a synchronized inflation shock, growth headwind, and
geopolitical risk premium that is re-pricing every asset class. Today's OPEC+
emergency meeting in Vienna faces a structural "deliverability gap" — Saudi
Arabia can theoretically raise output, but the blockade prevents that barrel
from reaching Western and Asian markets. No supply-side fix exists until the
Hormuz situation resolves. Meanwhile, Friday's March NFP (+178K, crushing
+59K estimate) delivered a labor market that, on the surface, looks resilient
— but with wages soft (+3.5% YoY) and the unemployment rate at 4.3%, the Fed
remains squarely on hold, watching oil-driven inflation eat into real incomes.

- Momentum: RSI readings across the board cluster in the 43–52 range
  (SPX 46, NDX 47, Gold 47, BTC 43, Silver 45) — neutral, neither oversold

  confirm fresh upside. The sole outlier is WTI at 71 — technically overbought

  Bullish on SPX, Gold, Silver, and WTI; Bearish on NDX and BTC — a subtle
  signal that the current equity bounce has more support in large-caps than
  tech, and that crypto remains under institutional distribution.

- Volatility: VIX at 23.87 is in contango (VIX3M at 24.72), which is the
  "normal" structure — markets are not pricing an imminent crash but are
  maintaining a healthy risk premium. WTI ATR% at 7.42% is the highest in our
  universe, confirming energy is the highest-risk, highest-reward trade. SPX
  ATR% of 1.61% signals modest realized vol despite the macro chaos. Gold's

  this relationship. Gold/DXY at -0.07 (near-zero) reflects gold trading on
  pure geopolitical fear rather than its traditional inverse dollar relationship.

  OI ratios even higher at 1.76/1.84) reflect institutional hedging, not
  outright panic. This is a market being sold into rallies by professionals,
  consistent with the bear-structure technicals. There is no capitulation signal
  — no VIX spike to 40+, no P/C > 2.0 — meaning the downtrend is likely
  orderly continuation rather than a crash flush.

compressing consumer staples margins, making traditional defensive positioning
less reliable. Utilities (XLU +1.65%) are beginning to attract interest as an
inflation-resistant yield play.

HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — March CPI (Friday, April 10, 8:30 AM ET):
This is the report of the week. The interplay of two opposing forces makes
positioning extremely difficult: the Hormuz supply shock has almost certainly
pushed headline CPI materially higher (WTI +95% YTD, energy's weight in CPI
~7%), while softer wage growth (+3.5% YoY, +0.2% MoM in March) and a cooling
services sector argue for modest core readings. Consensus is coalescing around
headline CPI +5.2–5.5% YoY (up from February's +3.8%) driven entirely by
energy. Core CPI is expected to print +3.4% YoY — above the Fed's 2% target
but showing no acceleration. The asymmetric risk: a hot core print (>3.6%)
would crush the one remaining rate-cut expectation for 2026, sending yields to
4.60 (the 52W high) and equities to retest YTD lows. A soft core print with
hot headline would confirm the "transitory energy shock" narrative and give
the Fed cover to stay put — mildly supportive for equities and very bullish
for Gold. Markets cannot price this cleanly; expect elevated vol around the
print and favor gamma-long positioning heading into Thursday close.
