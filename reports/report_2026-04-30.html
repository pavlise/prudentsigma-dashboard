HIGHEST IMPACT EVENT — FOMC Decision + March Core PCE (Today):

The Fed holds today with the market pricing near-zero probability of a cut.
The real binary is the tone. If core PCE prints at or above 2.8% YoY (vs.
the 2.6% prior), combined with the energy-driven inflation backdrop (WTI
+91% YTD), Powell will be forced to push back on any 2026 cut timeline —
a hawkish hold that could spike the 10Y yield toward the 4.60% 52-week
high and reprice NDX (currently at RSI 70) lower by 2-4%. Conversely, if
core PCE underwhelms at ≤2.4% and GDP comes in above consensus, risk assets
get a green light — the put overhang (P/C 1.33) unwinds fast. The
asymmetric scenario: a hotter-than-expected PCE print is the single largest
near-term risk to the current equity bull structure. Additionally, any
commentary on Powell's board position introduces a tail-risk governance
discount not yet reflected in credit spreads.
